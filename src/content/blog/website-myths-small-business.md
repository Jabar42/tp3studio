"Websites are dead — social media is all you need." "It's way too expensive and takes months." "My business is too small for a website."

If you're a small business owner, you've probably heard — or thought — some version of these. They're myths repeated so often they start to sound like truth. But the rules have changed.

Let's break down the 7 most common myths, one by one, with facts and common sense.

## Myth 1: "Social media is enough"

This is the most dangerous one, because it has a grain of truth. Yes, social media gives you visibility. But you don't own any of it.

Instagram, Facebook, or TikTok can change their algorithm overnight, and your reach plummets. If your account gets suspended — by mistake or by a false report — your entire digital business disappears with it. Your followers aren't yours: you rented them from a platform.

Your website is your home on the internet. Nobody can take it away, nobody changes the rules mid-game, and you control what happens there.

**The truth:** Social media brings people to your door. Your website welcomes them in.

## Myth 2: "It's too expensive for my business"

This myth comes from a time when a website cost 3 or 4 million pesos and required a developer, a designer, and hosting that felt like a utility bill.

Today you can have a professional website — with design, domain, hosting, and even a chatbot — for less than what you spend each month on delivery orders or digital ads. At Tp3studio, we start from $100 USD, and it's up and running in 48 hours.

**The truth:** Not having a website is more expensive. Every customer who looks for you and can't find you is money lost.

## Myth 3: "I need to know technology"

There's a natural confusion: building a website sounds like code, FTP, databases, and weird stuff. For the modern small business, that's in the past.

Today, websites are built with visual platforms. Like assembling IKEA furniture: the pieces come ready, you just arrange them. At Tp3studio, we handle everything: you just tell us what you want to communicate and we publish it.

**The truth:** If you know how to send a WhatsApp message, you have everything you need to get a website. We do the rest.

## Myth 4: "Nobody uses websites anymore — people search on social media"

This one is easy to debunk with one fact: **91% of people search for a business on Google before buying from them.** Not on Instagram, not on TikTok. On Google.

When someone searches "restaurant near me" or "hair salon in [your city]," what shows up isn't your Instagram post — it's web search results. If you don't have a website indexed on Google, you don't exist to that customer.

**The truth:** People search on Google. Your website is your business card in that search.

## Myth 5: "Having a website is complicated and requires constant maintenance"

Ten years ago, this was true. You had to update plugins, deal with security vulnerabilities, and if something broke on a Sunday, you were without a site until Monday.

Today, modern platforms (like the ones we use) handle security, updates, and performance automatically. It's like having a car that takes itself to the shop. No maintenance, no headaches.

**The truth:** A modern website maintains itself. You just focus on serving your customers well.

## Myth 6: "Better to wait until my business is bigger"

This is the saddest myth. Think about a physical store: if you open a restaurant, you put up a sign on day one, even if you only have 4 tables. You don't wait until you have 40 tables for people to know you exist.

The same goes for a website. The earlier you show up online, the faster you start building presence, credibility, and customers. Waiting to "grow" before getting a website is like not putting a roof on your shop because "I'll do it when I have more customers."

**The truth:** A website isn't a luxury for big businesses. It's a tool for growth, and the smaller the seed, the more it needs good soil to put down roots.

## Myth 7: "A website won't bring me more customers"

A website alone doesn't perform magic. But a well-made website, optimized for Google, with good information and clear contact options, is your 24/7 salesperson.

Your website works while you sleep, while you're out, while you're serving another customer at your location. It gets visits, answers questions (with a chatbot), captures leads, and turns them into appointments.

**The truth:** A website is the only investment that works for you without asking for a salary, without vacations, and without a schedule.
