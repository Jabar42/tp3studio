"I'll just stick with social media — everyone's there anyway." It's something we hear all the time. And it makes sense: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and WhatsApp are part of any business's daily life.

But there's a huge difference between **being where people hang out** and **having a place where people find you when they need something.**

Let's compare both options for what they are: tools. Each one serves a different purpose. The trick is knowing how to use each one.

## Social Media: The Storefront Window

Social media is great for one thing: **getting people to know you.** You post content, people comment, you share promotions, you interact. It's like having a stall at a busy fair: lots of people walk by, you can grab their attention, and if your content is good, some of them stop to ask questions.

**The good:**

- Fast reach. A good reel can reach thousands of people in hours.
- Direct interaction. People message you, comment, react. There's conversation.
- It's "free" (in theory). Opening an account doesn't cost money.
- Great for showing day-to-day life, behind the scenes, your business's personality.

**The bad:**

- **You don't own anything.** Your account, your followers, your reach — it all depends on a platform that can change the rules whenever it wants.
- The algorithm decides who sees you. One day you have 10K reach, the next day 500. No warning.
- If your account gets suspended (by mistake, false report, or because the algorithm glitched), **you disappear.** Without a website to send your customers to, you've lost everything.
- The competition for attention is brutal. You're competing with dancing cats, viral recipes, and celebrities.
- Conversion is low. Getting likes doesn't mean people are buying.

## Website: Your Own Home

A website is your address on the internet. It's the place you send people when you want them to take you seriously. It's your store, open 24/7, where you set the rules.

**The good:**

- **You own it.** Nobody takes your website away, nobody changes the algorithm, nobody decides who sees you.
- Your website works while you sleep. It gets visits, answers questions (with a chatbot), captures leads, books appointments. All automatic.
- People who land on your website are already interested. They didn't get there because an algorithm put it in front of them — they got there because they were looking for you.
- Google indexes your website. When someone searches "restaurant in [your city]" or "accountant near me," you show up in results. Social media posts don't show up on Google.
- You can have all the information you want: prices, services, hours, photos, testimonials, blog, FAQ. No character limits.

**The bad:**

- Requires an upfront investment. But today you can have a professional website from $100 USD, not the millions it used to cost.
- It doesn't have reach by itself. You need to drive people to your website (through social media, Google, WhatsApp, ads).
- You need to keep it updated (though modern platforms make this almost automatic).

## The Strategy That Actually Works

It's not about choosing one or the other. The winning formula is:

> **Social media to attract. Website to convert.**

Use social media to:

- Show your work, your process, your personality
- Post promotions and engaging content
- Answer quick questions
- Build trust with testimonials and real stories

Use your website to:

- Keep all your service information available 24/7
- Capture leads with forms and chatbots
- Close sales without intermediaries
- Show up on Google when people search for you
- Have a place you can always send people to

Every social media post should end with "and there's more on my website" or "link in bio to book." That's how you turn passing attention into real customers.

## A Real-World Example

Imagine you run a hair salon. You post an incredible before-and-after on Instagram. 500 likes, 50 comments, 10 people asking "how much does it cost?"

Without a website: you reply one by one. Some leave you on read, others ask for your address. At the end of the day, maybe 1 person booked.

With a website: you reply "thanks 😊 all the info is at [link] — prices, address, and you can book directly." All 10 people click through, see prices, location, hours. 3 book using the reservation button while you're replying to other messages.

The website worked for you.

## So Which One Should You Choose?

**Both.** But if you have to start with one, start with the website. The website is the foundation. Social media is the megaphone that brings people to your foundation.

Without a website, your social media is like a stall at a fair with no address: people see you, but then they don't know where to find you to buy from you. With a website, every like, every view, every comment can turn into a real customer.

And the best part: today, a professional website for your business costs less than what you spend on digital ads in a month. It's not a luxury — it's an investment that works for you 24 hours a day.
