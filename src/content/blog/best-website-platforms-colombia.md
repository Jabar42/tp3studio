## 2. Wix — Easiest to DIY

**Who it's for:** Entrepreneurs with time and willingness to learn a visual builder.

**Price:** From $27 USD/month (Combo plan) up to $159 USD/month (Business VIP). In Colombia, with VAT and exchange rates, you end up paying between roughly $110,000 and $650,000 COP per month.

**Pros:**
- Very intuitive drag-and-drop interface.
- Hundreds of templates to choose from.
- Can have something functional in a couple of days.
- Includes hosting and domain for one year on annual plans.

**Cons:**
- Drag-and-drop designs look generic. Your site looks like many others.
- Monthly cost in Colombia is high with the current exchange rate.
- Doesn't include a chatbot. You have to integrate one separately (and pay extra).
- SEO isn't as good as WordPress. Google doesn't favor them as much.
- Migrating your site to another platform later is very difficult. Wix locks you in.
- No local support in Colombia. If something breaks, you talk to a bot in English.

**Estimated annual cost in Colombia:** $330,000 — $1,900,000 COP/year.

## 3. WordPress — Most Powerful (But Takes Work)

**Who it's for:** People with patience, time to learn, or budget to hire a developer.

**Price:** The software is free. You pay hosting ($8-20 USD/month) + domain ($10-15 USD/year) + templates and plugins (variable). Total first year: roughly $300,000 to $1,500,000 COP, plus your time.

**Pros:**
- Total control over every aspect of your site.
- Thousands of templates and plugins to customize.
- Best SEO in the market (with plugins like Yoast or RankMath).
- You own everything: you can migrate your site to any hosting anytime.
- Scalable: start small and grow to a massive site.

**Cons:**
- **Requires constant maintenance.** Updating plugins, fixing conflicts, managing security. It's not "set it and forget it."
- The learning curve is steep. The first few weeks are slow.
- If something breaks (incompatible plugin, hack), you need to know how to fix it or pay someone.
- Doesn't include chatbot, SEO, or anything. You add everything yourself or pay separately.
- If you hire a developer to set it up and hand it over, you're looking at $500,000 — $2,000,000 COP just for setup.

**Estimated annual cost in Colombia:** $300,000 — $1,500,000 COP + your time (or developer fees).

## 4. Shopify — King of Online Stores

**Who it's for:** Businesses selling physical products online that need a full e-commerce store.

**Price:** From $39 USD/month (Basic Shopify). In Colombia, that's roughly $160,000 COP/month. The most popular plan ($105 USD/month) hits $430,000 COP/month.

**Pros:**
- Built specifically for selling products online.
- Inventory management, payments, shipping — all integrated.
- Professional e-commerce templates.
- Apps for practically any feature you need.
- Integrated payment processing (with transaction fees).

**Cons:**
- **Expensive for a small business.** The basic plan plus the apps you'll need (each app charges separately) and transaction fees add up fast.
- Not designed for service businesses. If you sell services, you're overpaying for features you don't use.
- Transaction fees (2.4% - 2.9% + $0.30 USD) eat into margins on low-priced products.
- You pay in dollars. With Colombia's exchange rate, every month is a gamble.
- No chatbot included. You have to add one with an app (extra cost).

**Estimated annual cost in Colombia:** $1,900,000 — $5,200,000 COP/year + transaction fees.
